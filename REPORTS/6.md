# EvalFlow Sprint 5 Report: Financial Ledger & Monetization

---

## Overview
Sprint 5 introduces a complete monetization and financial ledger subsystem into EvalFlow. It transforms the platform into an economy where students purchase credits to take exams, evaluators earn real-world currency for their grading efforts, and administrators track system revenue and process payouts. 

This sprint integrates the SSLCommerz payment gateway for student deposits and establishes strict server-side validation for credit consumption and withdrawal limits.

## Features Implemented

### 💳 Student Subsystem: My Wallet
- **Top-Up Credits (SSLCommerz)** — Students have a dedicated "My Wallet" page where they can initiate a deposit. The system redirects to the SSLCommerz sandbox. Upon a successful transaction (or mock fallback if sandbox credentials are not configured), the student's `balance` is credited. 
- **Pay-Per-Exam (Credit Consumption)** — Answer scripts are no longer free to upload. When a student attempts to upload a script, the system calculates the exam fee (1 Credit = 1 Exam Total Mark). If the student's balance is sufficient, the fee is deducted and logged as an `exam_fee` transaction. If not, they are blocked and prompted to top up.
- **Transaction History** — Students can view a tabular history of their deposits and exam fees.

### 💰 Evaluator Subsystem: Earnings & Withdrawals
- **Automated Earnings** — When an evaluator grades a script for the *first time* (status changes from pending to evaluated), they automatically earn `0.75 TK` for every 1 total mark of the exam. The earning is credited to their `balance` and logged as an `earning` transaction.
- **Withdrawal Requests** — Evaluators have an "Earnings" page showing their available balance and all-time earnings. They can request a withdrawal to mobile financial services (bKash, Nagad) or banks (DBBL).
- **Minimum Withdrawal Enforced** — A dedicated middleware (`EnsureMinimumWithdrawal`) strictly enforces that evaluators cannot request withdrawals under `100 TK`. 

### 💼 Admin Subsystem: Financial Dashboard
- **Revenue Overview** — Admins have a new "Finance" dashboard that calculates the Total Student Deposits, Total Evaluator Earnings, and the **Net System Revenue**.
- **Withdrawal Management** — Admins see a paginated table of all pending evaluator withdrawal requests. After manually transferring the money via the requested method (e.g., sending bKash), the Admin clicks **"Approve & Clear"**, which finalizes the withdrawal and marks the underlying transaction as completed.

## Middleware / Validation Changes
| Validation | Location | What It Does |
|-----------|---------|-------------|
| `withdraw.min` | `EnsureMinimumWithdrawal.php` | Rejects any withdrawal POST request where `amount < 100`. Returns HTTP 302 back with error. |
| CSRF Exception | `bootstrap/app.php` | Exempts `/student/finance/*` routes from CSRF token validation to allow SSLCommerz to POST callback data successfully. |
| Sufficient Balance | `StudentExamController@uploadScript` | Checks `if ($user->balance < $exam->total_marks)` before allowing a PDF upload. |

|--------|-----|-------------|------|
| GET | `/student/finance` | View student wallet and transaction history | `student` |

## How to Test (End-to-End)

1. **Top Up as Student:** Log in as Student. Go to "My Wallet" and deposit 500 TK. The system will mock the SSLCommerz redirect and credit your balance.
2. **Take Exam (Consume Credit):** Go to an active exam. Attempt to upload your script. If the exam is 50 marks, 50 TK will be deducted from your balance. 
3. **Earn as Evaluator:** Log out and log in as the assigned Evaluator. Go to "My Scripts" and grade the student's paper. Upon saving, go to "Earnings". You will see an earning of `50 marks * 0.75 = 37.50 TK`.
4. **Try to Withdraw (Fail):** Try to request a withdrawal for your 37.50 TK. The system will block it because it's under 100 TK.
5. **Request Valid Withdrawal:** (Assume you graded more papers and have >100 TK). Request a withdrawal to your bKash number.
6. **Approve as Admin:** Log in as Admin. Go to "Finance". You will see the Net Revenue and the Evaluator's pending request. Click "Approve & Clear".
